Our Mission

The mission of Project Alpha of LA is to inspire young males to reach their fullest potential as thoughtful leaders, successful professionals and engaged citizens.

Our Vision

We envision a yearly cohort of ambitious and committed young males who positively embrace their role in healthy relationships; who actively thrive to achieve academically, civicly, professionally and socially across diverse environments.

ABOUT

PROJECT ALPHA OF LA

Project Alpha of LA is a 501(c)3 not-for-profit organization founded and powered by the Mu Sigma Lambda Chapter (Baldwin Hills/Culver City) of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc.

Project Alpha of LA was established in 19xx by the Alpha Brothers of Mu Sigma Lambda chapter for purposes of providing mentorship and scholarships to young people in greater Los Angeles and to provide those young people with skills and tools needed for healthy outcomes in their relationships, on campus and in the community.

Alpha Phi Alpha

Alpha Phi Alpha is the first intercollegiate fraternity to be incorporated in the U.S. established by and for Black men, founded in 1906 in Ithaca, New York on the campus of Cornell University by seven Founders known as the Seven Jewels.

Mu Sigma Lambda Chapter

The Mu Sigma Lambda Chapter of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. was chartered in 1977 and serves the communities of Baldwin Hills, Culver City and greater Los Angeles.

Programs

Alpha Junior Gents

Alpha Junior Gents utilizes a group mentoring format targeting young males in South Los Angeles between ages 12-18 years with a primary emphasis to build character and confidence among our future leaders and professionals.

Specifically, the aim of the program is to provide meaningful life skills in addition to the tools required to achieve academic, social and economic success into adulthood.

For more information, visit juniorgents.org.

Scholarships

Each year, three to six deserving male high school seniors enrolling at a 4-year college or university receive a scholarship directly from Project Alpha of LA, Inc ranging from $1,000 to $2,000.

Students are eligible to reapply as rising sophomores, juniors, and seniors.

Events

Used Book Sales

Project Alpha of LA, Inc. hosts a monthly used book fair in the community, with 100% of profits supporting the Alpha Junior Gents and Scholarship programs.

Donations are tax-deductible.

LA Derby Day

The 5th Annual LA Derby Day will be held on Saturday, May 2, 2020 from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM at Santa Anita Park in Arcadia, California. LA Derby Day is our biggest celebration and fundraiser of the year. The annual event brings together over 500 guests, a mostly college educated, middle class and professional African American audience, the majority of them supporters of Project Alpha of LA, Inc.

For more information, visit www.laderbyday.com.
